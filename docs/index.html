The six Excel mistakes this toolkit prevents

Over 3 years auditing 40+ ISO/IEC 17025 calibration laboratories, I keep finding the same six mistakes in measurement uncertainty budgets. ISO 10012:2026 makes each one more audit-relevant than the 2003 edition did.

  • Bessel skip — STDEVP (n) used instead of STDEV (n-1) for sample standard deviation
  • k-divide skip — Certificate value "U = 0.5 nm at k = 2" entered as standard uncertainty instead of being divided by k
  • Default k = 2 instead of Welch-Satterthwaite + Student-t coverage factor
  • Correlated components treated as independent in root-sum-of-squares
  • Sensitivity coefficients defaulted to 1 for non-linear measurement models
  • Expanded U reported without k and νeff — forbidden by ISO 10012:2026 clause 6.2

What an output looks like (CD-SEM 45 nm)

The Excel template's CDSEM_45nm worked example produces a complete budget for a 45 nm critical-dimension measurement combining Type A repeatability, magnification calibration, and line-edge roughness.

Mean (nm): 45.1125 Std (Bessel): 0.0245 n (repeats): 20 u_A (Type A): 0.0055 nm (= s / √n, ν = 19) u_B magnification: 0.2500 nm (= 0.5 / 2, ν = 50) u_B line-edge LER: 0.2000 nm (= 0.4 / 2, ν = 50) ───────────────────────────────────── u_c (combined): 0.3202 nm ν_eff (Welch-Satterthwaite): 95.5 k @ 95% (Student-t): 1.985 U (expanded): 0.636 nm Reported result (ISO 10012:2026 §6.2 format): CD = 45.11 ± 0.64 nm @ 95% (k = 1.99, ν_eff = 95)

5-minute workflow

  1. Open GUM_Toolkit_v1.xlsx. Pick a worked-example sheet or start from BLANK_TEMPLATE.
  2. Enter measurand name, units, and confidence level in cells B2–B4.
  3. Fill the yellow input cells: symbol, value, divisor (k or √3), degrees of freedom.
  4. Read uc, νeff, k, U from the green cells. Cell B17 generates the canonical "y ± U at L% confidence (k = …, νeff = …)" string.
  5. Cross-check the 24-point KOLAS audit checklist. Done.
